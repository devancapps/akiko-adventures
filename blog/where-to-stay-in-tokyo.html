Best Areas to Stay in Tokyo

Choosing where to stay in Tokyo can be overwhelming. We've broken down the best areas based on your interests and budget:

Top Neighborhoods

  • Shinjuku - Best for first-time visitors
  • Shibuya - Perfect for shopping and nightlife
  • Asakusa - Budget-friendly, traditional area
  • Tokyo Station - Ideal for business travelers

Money-Saving Tips

  1. Book in Advance - Hotels fill up quickly, especially during peak seasons
  2. Consider Business Hotels - Clean, efficient, and budget-friendly
  3. Stay Near a Metro Station - Save on transportation costs
  4. Look for Included Breakfast - Can save $10-15 per day

Transportation Access

When choosing your hotel, consider these transportation factors:

  • Distance to nearest metro station
  • Access to Yamanote Line (Tokyo's circular railway)
  • Airport transfer options
  • Walking distance to major attractions

Pro Tips

  • Most hotels offer free luggage storage after check-out
  • Business hotels often have coin laundry facilities
  • Look for hotels with mobile Wi-Fi rentals
  • Consider capsule hotels for unique experiences

Best Time to Book

Book your Tokyo hotel at least 3 months in advance, especially during these peak periods:

  • Cherry Blossom Season (Late March - Early April)
  • Golden Week (Late April - Early May)
  • Fall Foliage Season (November)
  • New Year's Holiday (December 28 - January 4)
