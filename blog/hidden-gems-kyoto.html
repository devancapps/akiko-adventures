10 Hidden Gems in Kyoto's Ancient Streets

March 15, 2024 8 min read

Find Hotels in Kyoto

Introduction

While most tourists flock to famous spots like Kinkaku-ji and Fushimi Inari, Kyoto's true magic lies in its hidden corners. We've explored the ancient capital's lesser-known spots to bring you these authentic experiences away from the crowds.

Quick Tips

  • Visit early morning or evening to avoid crowds
  • Use Google Maps to save these locations
  • Consider renting a bicycle
  • Learn basic Japanese greetings

Hidden Temples

1. Otagi Nenbutsu-ji Temple

Home to 1,200 unique stone statues, each with its own personality. Located in the peaceful Arashiyama district, this temple sees few visitors despite its incredible charm.

2. Shōren-in Temple

A peaceful temple known for its beautiful garden and night illuminations. Perfect for meditation and tea ceremony experiences.

Local Markets & Shopping Streets

3. Nishiki Tenmangu Shrine

Hidden above Nishiki Market, this peaceful shrine offers a quiet escape from the bustling shopping street below.

4. Shinkyogoku Shopping Street

Less touristy than Teramachi, this traditional shopping arcade offers local crafts and authentic souvenirs.

Best Times to Visit

  • Early Morning (7-9 AM): Temples and shrines
  • Late Morning (10 AM-12 PM): Markets
  • Afternoon (2-4 PM): Tea houses
  • Evening (After 5 PM): Gardens and illuminations

Hidden Food Spots

5. Demachi Masugata Shopping Arcade

A local food market where Kyoto's residents shop. Try the fresh mochi, pickled vegetables, and seasonal specialties.

6. Ninen-zaka Tea Houses

Skip the crowded main street and explore the quiet alleys behind Ninen-zaka for authentic tea houses and local cafes.

Secret Gardens

7. Shusuitei Tea Garden

A hidden garden in the Arashiyama district, perfect for peaceful contemplation away from the bamboo forest crowds.

8. Murin-an Villa

A stunning garden designed by famous landscape architect Ogawa Jihei. Features a beautiful stream and mountain views.

Off-the-Beaten-Path Walks

9. Philosopher's Path Alternative

Explore the quiet residential streets parallel to the famous Philosopher's Path for a more authentic experience.

10. Kiyamachi Street at Night

While tourists crowd Pontocho, locals prefer the atmospheric Kiyamachi Street for evening walks and dining.

Getting Around

  • Rent a bicycle for easy exploration
  • Use the reliable bus network
  • Consider a one-day bus pass
  • Download offline maps

Photography Tips

These hidden spots offer unique photo opportunities without the usual crowds. Best times for photography:

  • Early morning for misty temple grounds
  • Golden hour for traditional streets
  • Rainy days for moody shots
  • Night time for illuminated gardens
