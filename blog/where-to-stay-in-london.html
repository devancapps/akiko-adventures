Where to Stay in London – Complete 2024 Guide

March 20, 2024 8 min read

Find Your Perfect London Hotel

London's diverse neighborhoods each offer a unique experience for visitors. Whether you're seeking historic charm, trendy vibes, or budget-friendly options, this guide will help you find your perfect base in the British capital.

Quick Tips

  • Book accommodations near a Tube station for easy access to attractions
  • Consider staying in Zones 1-2 to minimize transportation costs
  • Check our travel resources for the best booking platforms
  • Compare prices across different seasons (peak: June-August)

Best Areas to Stay in London

Covent Garden & Soho

Perfect for first-time visitors and theater lovers

  • Central location
  • Vibrant nightlife
  • Walking distance to attractions

South Kensington

Ideal for families and culture enthusiasts

  • Major museums nearby
  • Beautiful parks
  • Safe and quiet area

Budget-Friendly Areas

Looking to save on accommodation? Consider these neighborhoods:

  • Paddington - Great transport links and reasonable prices
  • Kings Cross - Excellent value and regenerated area
  • Southwark - Walking distance to attractions south of the Thames
  • Shoreditch - Hip area with boutique hostels and hotels

Recommended Hotels by Budget

Luxury (£200+)

  • The Savoy - Historic luxury on the Strand
  • Shangri-La The Shard - Stunning views of London
  • The Connaught - Classic Mayfair elegance

Mid-Range (£100-200)

  • citizenM Tower of London - Modern and stylish
  • The Hoxton, Shoreditch - Trendy and well-located
  • Park Plaza Westminster Bridge - Great views of Big Ben

Budget (Under £100)

  • Point A Hotels - Clean and compact rooms
  • Premier Inn - Reliable UK chain with good locations
  • YHA London Central - Modern hostel with private rooms

Transportation Tips

When choosing your accommodation, consider:

  • Distance to nearest Tube station
  • Number of Tube lines serving the area
  • Night bus routes for late returns
  • Walking distance to main attractions

Pro Tips

  • Get an Oyster card for public transport
  • Consider hotels with airport shuttle service
  • Check if breakfast is included (can save £10-15/day)
  • Look for hotels with free cancellation options

Best Time to Book

Book your London hotel at least 2-3 months in advance, especially during:

  • Summer Season (June-August)
  • Christmas and New Year
  • School Holidays
  • Major Events (Wimbledon, London Fashion Week)
